# The Olympic Halo: Measuring Spatial Spillovers of the 2026 Milan-Cortina Games on Mountain Real Estate

## 💡 The Project Idea
Mega-events like the Winter Olympics bring massive infrastructure investments and global prestige to host cities. While the direct economic impact on the epicenter is well-documented, the "spatial spillover"—how this economic shock ripples outward to neighboring towns—is rarely measured with precision. 

This project investigates the real estate market of Northern Italy following the June 2019 announcement of the 2026 Milan-Cortina Winter Olympics. Using highly granular, neighborhood-level data, this research aims to prove whether proximity to Cortina d'Ampezzo caused a measurable, proportional premium on house prices in the surrounding mountain municipalities. 

By treating the distance to Cortina as a continuous variable, this project moves beyond simple "treated vs. untreated" zones to map the exact geographic decay of an Olympic economic shock.

## 🎯 Core Research Questions
* Did the 2019 Olympic announcement create a structural break in the trajectory of mountain real estate prices?
* How does the "Olympic Premium" decay as distance from the host city increases?
* At what exact kilometer distance does the spillover effect statistically vanish?
* Are these price dynamics driven by a genuine regional spillover, or are they exclusively fueled by extreme luxury market behavior inside Cortina itself?

## 🧬 Methodological Approach
To cleanly isolate the Olympic effect from general macroeconomic trends (like the post-COVID mountain housing boom), this project utilizes a **Continuous Difference-in-Differences (DiD) framework**.

* **The Sample:** Restricted exclusively to officially designated mountain municipalities in the Triveneto region. This guarantees a clean control group that shares the exact same baseline alpine characteristics.
* **The Treatment:** A continuous least-cost path distance metric, calculating the exact driving distance from every local real estate zone to Cortina.
* **The Controls:** High-dimensional fixed effects (Zone and Province-by-Semester) completely absorb local neighborhood traits and macro-regional policy changes.

## 🗺️ Project Roadmap

### Phase 1: Data Acquisition & Harmonization
* Download semester-level micro-data from the Osservatorio del Mercato Immobiliare (OMI) covering 2015-2026.
* Extract official demographic and terrain classifications from ISTAT.
* Pull municipal tax data (IRPEF) from the Ministry of Economy and Finance.
* Standardize all geographic keys using official ISTAT municipality codes to ensure clean merging.

### Phase 2: Spatial Engineering & Filtering
* Filter the national dataset to isolate the Triveneto region.
* Apply ISTAT altitude classifications to drop all flatland and coastal municipalities.
* Manually exclude secondary 2026 Olympic clusters (e.g., Val di Fiemme, Anterselva) to prevent control group contamination.
* Calculate the exact least-cost driving distance from each OMI zone to Cortina d'Ampezzo.

### Phase 3: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* Plot the Pre-2019 vs. Post-2019 average house price growth rates against distance for the Province of Belluno.
* Generate preliminary visual proofs of the parallel trends assumption using a binary distance cutoff.
* Identify and clean any spatial outliers or failed data merges.

### Phase 4: Econometric Modeling
* Estimate the baseline Two-Way Fixed Effects (TWFE) regression.
* Run an Event Study (Dynamic DiD) interacting distance with every single semester to mathematically prove pre-announcement parallel trends.
* Execute the "Doughnut Specification" robustness check by dropping Cortina from the sample to prove the spillover exists independently of the epicenter.

### Phase 5: Synthesis & Reporting
* Format regression outputs into publication-ready tables.
* Polish continuous distance gradient charts.
* Draft the final research paper, focusing on the literature gap filled by applying spatial micro-econometrics to the 2026 Winter Games.
